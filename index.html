The Problem: Widespread Inefficiency

Our initial analysis found that a vast majority of bins were severely underutilized, leading to wasted resources and inefficient collection routes.

The Solution: Strategic Consolidation

By removing underused bins and consolidating them in central, high-traffic areas, we created a much more balanced and efficient system.

Our Four-Step Process to Success

A simple, effective, and data-backed methodology.

1.

Data Collection

Tracked usage of all 215 bins.

2.

Analysis

Identified low, medium, and high usage bins.

3.

Consolidation

Removed 60 underperforming bins.

4.

Optimization

Relocated bins to central, high-traffic hubs.

Departmental Impact

The optimization strategy was tailored to each department's needs, with the largest reductions occurring in areas with the most potential for centralization.

This project serves as a model for future campus-wide efficiency initiatives.
